# 20-Year Founder Succession Roadmap

## Overview

This roadmap defines the gradual, reversible transition of RCOLLAB from founder-led to institutionally governed, ensuring stability at every stage. No premature decentralization. No hostile takeover vulnerability. No governance capture.

---

## Phase 1 — Founder High Control (Years 0–5)

### Authority Model
- Founder retains full strategic authority
- All critical decisions logged via `logFounderDecision()`
- Override capability preserved but monitored

### Objectives
- Escrow engine hardened to institutional grade
- Ledger immutability verified and audited
- Governance advisory pods formed (non-binding)
- Risk workflow operational — all changes evaluated
- Founder Risk Exposure Score tracked monthly

### Override Decay Curve
- Year 0: Unlimited overrides (all logged)
- Year 1: Override frequency alerts at >5/week
- Year 2: High-risk overrides require justification (>20 chars)
- Year 3: Critical overrides require 24h delay
- Year 4: Escrow overrides require multi-party review
- Year 5: Reserve overrides require governance pod acknowledgment

### Risk Caps
- Maximum founder sole-decisions: No cap (but tracked)
- Power Concentration Index target: < 70
- Dependency Score target: < 80

### Stability Metrics
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Escrow invariant pass rate | 100% |
| Ledger integrity | 100% |
| Founder decision log completeness | > 95% |

---

## Phase 2 — Institutional Maturity (Years 5–10)

### Authority Model
- Governance pods gain binding vote on medium-risk decisions
- Founder retains veto on high/critical decisions
- Multi-party review mandatory for financial mutations > $100K

### Objectives
- Credit system stability proven over 3+ years
- Liquidity resilience tested under stress
- Succession Readiness Score > 40
- At least 3 qualified institutional leaders identified
- Emergency continuity protocol tested annually

### Override Decay Curve
- Year 5: All high-risk overrides require governance pod notification
- Year 6: Governance veto requires public justification
- Year 7: Reserve issuance requires 2-of-3 approval
- Year 8: Bond framework changes require institutional advisory
- Year 9: Capital activation requires multi-party sign-off
- Year 10: Founder override limited to critical-only

### Risk Caps
- Power Concentration Index target: < 55
- Dependency Score target: < 60
- Strategic Drift Index target: < 30

---

## Phase 3 — Distributed Governance (Years 10–15)

### Authority Model
- Governance pods have binding authority on most decisions
- Founder retains emergency override only
- Constitutional amendments require supermajority + founder consent

### Objectives
- Reserve issuance shared authority (3-of-5 approval)
- Risk tier approval fully multi-party
- Autonomous monitoring handles 90%+ of operational decisions
- Succession Readiness Score > 65
- System operates normally during 30-day founder absence tests

### Override Decay Curve
- Year 10: Founder overrides limited to emergency-only
- Year 12: Emergency overrides require 48h post-review
- Year 14: Override attempts trigger automatic stability simulation

### Risk Caps
- Power Concentration Index target: < 40
- Dependency Score target: < 40
- Founder sole-decision rate target: < 20%

---

## Phase 4 — System Autonomy (Years 15–20)

### Authority Model
- Constitutional core immutable (requires 80% supermajority to amend)
- Founder role transitions to "Protocol Guardian" (advisory, non-operational)
- Governance fully distributed across institutional stakeholders
- Emergency protocols self-activating

### Objectives
- System self-heals without founder intervention
- All financial operations multi-party by default
- Succession Readiness Score > 85
- Power Concentration Index < 25
- Founder Dependency Score < 15

### Override Decay Curve
- Year 15: Founder override = protocol guardian veto (1 per quarter max)
- Year 17: Guardian veto requires constitutional justification
- Year 20: Founder authority = symbolic + constitutional guardian role

## Emergency Fallback Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Founder Sudden Unavailability
1. System detects 72h inactivity
2. Advisory notification to governance pod
3. 7-day grace period
4. Emergency continuity mode activates
5. High-risk modules frozen
6. Escrow/ledger/wallet preserved
7. No strategic expansion until resolution

### Scenario 2: Founder-Governance Conflict
1. Conflict logged and escalated
2. 48h cooling period enforced
3. Independent mediator activated
4. Constitutional review if unresolved
5. Platform Constitution supersedes all parties

### Scenario 3: Hostile Takeover Attempt
1. Anti-capture safeguards activate
2. Governance pod emergency session
3. Capital operations frozen
4. Constitutional protection enforced
5. Founder guardian veto preserved

### Scenario 4: Regulatory Forced Leadership Change
1. Stability buffer activates
2. Regulatory compliance mode engaged
3. Continuity protocol ensures operational stability
4. Successor appointed per succession criteria
5. 90-day transition period enforced
