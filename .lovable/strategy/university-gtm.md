# RCollab — University Go-To-Market Strategy

## Entry Strategy
Target: Dean of Engineering, FYP Director, Innovation Head, Career Services, R&D Office
Position as: "Escrow-backed Final Year Project Execution Infrastructure"
Use "infrastructure" language, avoid "marketplace" language.

## Pilot Program
- 3-6 month pilot: 10-30 FYPs, 2-5 sponsors
- Weekly analytics, milestone metrics, dispute tracking, SLA tracking

## Faculty Champion Model
- 1-2 faculty per university as internal champions
- Free premium dashboard, early access, recognition badge

## Sponsor Attraction Loop
- Local tech companies, corporates, innovation labs, government R&D
- After 1-2 successful projects → case study → attract more sponsors

## 12-Month Campus Expansion
- Months 0-3: 3-5 pilot universities, 20-50 projects, 5-10 sponsors
- Months 3-6: 10-15 universities, 150+ projects, case studies
- Months 6-9: 20-30 universities, 400+ projects
- Months 9-12: 50+ universities, subscription conversion begins

## Objection Handling
- "Too complex" → Pilot support + onboarding
- "We use spreadsheets" → SLA dashboard + escrow protection
- "We don't handle money" → Sponsor funds directly into escrow
- "Students might not complete" → Milestone structure reduces risk
- "Legal concerns" → Compliance docs + audit export

## National Expansion (Post-Regional Dominance)
- Approach national education boards, innovation ministries, government R&D
- Scale region by region, never globally at once
